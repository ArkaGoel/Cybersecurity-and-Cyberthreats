DOS Attacks

A denial of service attack or DOS is meant to shut down a machine or network making it inaccesible to intend all users temporarily

There are 2 general methods of DOS attack which are : Flood attacks crashing services. Flood attacks occur when the system receives too much traffic for the server to buffer, causing them to slow down and eventually stop

The 3 Popular types of flood attacks are-

  • Buffer overflow attacks - the most common DOS attack. The concept is to send more traffic to a network address than the programmers have built the system to handle.
  • ICMP flood - An Internal Control Message Protocol flood DDoS attack also known as ping flood attack which sends many echo requests to a targeted device.
